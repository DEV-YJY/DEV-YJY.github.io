What is Emotional Intelligence?

- Generally, intelligence is the ability to learn new concepts and apply your knowledge to problems. Emotional intelligence (EQ) is similar but it is the ability to learn and understand about yourself as well as the others. Daniel Goleman, an American psychologist, suggest that EQ consists of 5 components – Self-regulation, Motivation, Empathy and Social skills.


How is it different to IQ?

- As described before, EQ accounts for how you understand yourself and other people e.g. their feelings. Intelligence quotient (IQ) on the other focuses on measuring intelligence that includes analytical, problem-solving abilities and logic. Since it is designed to measure intellectual intelligence, it is given as a score from a series of tests (Higher IQ = better ability to learn). It has been known that higher academic scores are achieved by people with higher IQ than those with lower IQ scores without exerting more effort.


Why is it important?

- Although IQ is a good indication that measures peoples’ ability to learn it has no connection with how people understand the emotions of their own and other people’s.
